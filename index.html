Demi, who announced she had been diagnosed with thyroid cancer in May, first discovered the lump on her neck aged 20 at university in 2019 but was told that it was a 'harmless nodule' following a scan, ultrasound and blood test.

During this time, the reality star's stepfather Adrian, who was aged 46 at the time, was diagnosed with incurable Stage 4 melanoma with months left to live - after he first went to a doctor 18 months earlier with a lump on his ear.  

After appearing on the 2020 series of Love Island, the reality star said that eagle-eyed fans had warned her to get her lump checked out and she decided to do so  after it got bigger - but her appointment was delayed six times over seven months.

Demi thought she had nothing 'to really worry about' due to her past tests being 'completely clear', saying: 'Even today I don't know why the ultrasound didn't show anything.'

Despite being sent away again, the reality star insisted for more tests after what had happened to her stepfather Adrian and they drained fluid from her lump for testing.

Demi said that she 'wasn't worried' at the time but was left in 'complete shock' when she returned two weeks later to a consultant telling her it could be thyroid cancer and it had to be removed as 'quickly as possible'.

Two weeks later, Demi had the lump removed and tested, she recalled returning to hospital three weeks later for the results where she knew 'instantly' that she had cancer after seeing a Macmillan nurse which left her 'feeling quite numb'. 

The reality star has papillary thyroid cancer, she has been told that it is slow-growing and treatable. Demi will need surgery to remove her entire thyroid and she will also take hormone tablets for the rest of her life.

Demi - who said she wants to raise awareness of thyroid cancer and 'appreciate everything so much more' - also touched upon the two-inch scar she now has on her neck, calling it 'a small price to pay'. 

It comes after Demi candidly discussed how she had to be very persistent to get tested after her appointment was pushed back by seven months because of lockdown during a recent interview on Good Morning Britain. 

Demi told told hosts Susanna Reid and Bill Turnbull: 'It's been a bit of a hard thing to process, it's all very new to me and it's happening very quickly but I'm okay, that's the main thing.'

The brunette beauty also revealed that her appointment was pushed back from September to April during the pandemic.

WHAT IS THYROID CANCER?

Thyroid cancer is a rare type of cancer that affects the thyroid gland, a small gland at the base of the neck that produces hormones.

Women are two to three times more likely to develop it than men.

Symptoms of thyroid cancer can include:

  • a painless lump or swelling in the front of the neck
  • swollen glands in the neck
  • unexplained hoarseness that doesn't get better after a few weeks
  • a sore throat that doesn't get better
  • difficulty swallowing

Around 9 in every 10 people are alive five years after diagnosis. Many of these are cured and will have a normal lifespan.
